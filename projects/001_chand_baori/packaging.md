## Title (COMMITTED)

> **How Did India Build a Staircase That Chases the Water?**

- **Formula:** §4 #1 — the Question Hook (`How Did [Civilization] [Impossible-Sounding Feat]?`). Highest-CTR pattern for the "How They Built It" pillar.
- **Why this one:** it leads with the *engineering insight*, not the tourist fact. "A staircase that chases the water" is the seasonal moving-water-table story (§7, §9) compressed to four words — the paradox *is* the curiosity gap. This is the differentiation §4a #1 demands: an engineer's framing, not "3,500 mesmerizing steps" (what every listicle already says).
- **Supersedes** the working title in `research_notes.md` ("The 3,500-Step Staircase Built Into the Earth") — that was a descriptive statement, not a hook, and buried the insight.

**Alternate (number-forward, if A/B testing later):** "Why Does This Indian Well Have 3,500 Steps?" — pair with a `HOW DEEP?` thumbnail so the number isn't duplicated.

## Thumbnail concept (COMMITTED)

> **Dominant object:** the plunging diamond-lattice of double-flight steps (the iconic Chand Baori corner view), glowing warm saffron `#D4812A` against a dark, moody background — the pattern falling away steeply to imply bottomless depth.
> **Words (≤4):** **`3,500 STEPS`**

- **object + words →** "the plunging saffron step-lattice + `3,500 STEPS`".
- **Complement, not duplicate:** the title carries the *why* (chases the water); the thumbnail carries the *scale* (3,500). Viewer sees an impossible-looking lattice, reads an impossible-looking number, and the title tells them there's an engineering reason. Click.
- **Passes the intent of the 120-px squint test:** one dominant object (the lattice) ≥60% of frame, a hard number that reads at inbox size, curiosity gap intact (why so many? how deep? — the video answers).
- **A/B axis for Session 5b (vary ONE):** crop tightness — (a) full corner with sky context, (b) tight on the lattice only, (c) cross-section cutaway with the waterline. Best validated still is a legitimate base; punch contrast/saturation rather than regenerate.
